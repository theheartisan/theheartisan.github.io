At the time, the design was no less an aesthetic marvel than an engineering one. Baker Street station, and the others that opened, made up the first subterranean railway in the world.

The design features include ventilation shafts located in the arched ceiling, which allowed steam to escape and light to enter. In addition, gas globe lamps hanging from the ceiling provided a warm glow and visibility as trains were entering and leaving the station.
